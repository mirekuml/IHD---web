SAFE AND SECURE

Advanced architecture, essential safeguards

IHD protects the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of personal health information (PHI) through physical, technical, and administrative safeguards — compliant with international data legislations and standards.

01

Technical Safeguards

Advanced technologies — encryption, blockchain, anonymization, authentication, and cyber-threat protection — combined to achieve the most secure healthcare platform.

02

Physical Safeguards

Physical records and electronic devices containing PHI are kept under lock and key, ensuring sensitive health data is never physically exposed.

03

Administrative Safeguards

Strict access controls limit who can view PHI. All policies comply with international data legislations and standards.

04

Privacy by Design

Security and privacy are the default setting. Acutely sensitive client data stays confidential — including from employers — protected by enterprise-grade technologies in one solution.

HEALTH SECTOR THREAT OVERVIEW

Why healthcare needs stronger cyber security

Cyber security protects the devices we all use and the services we access from theft or damage — and prevents unauthorised access to the personal information stored on them. In the health sector, incidents can have devastating impacts on organisations and individuals, including threat to life.

Threats NCSC · ACSC

BEC and Ransomware

Business email compromise and ransomware present high-impact threats to the health sector and its medical transport and supply chains, as cybercriminals adapt to new technologies and increase their attacks.

Target ACSC

Why Health Is a Target

Highly sensitive personal data, valuable intellectual property, critical services, pressure to rapidly restore continuity, and public trust make the health sector a valuable and vulnerable target.

Impact 2020

The Cost of an Attack

In 2020, 92 ransomware attacks affected over 600 clinics, hospitals, and organisations and more than 18 million patient records — at an estimated cost of almost $21 billion.

Sources: National Cyber Security Centre (UK) · Australian Cyber Security Centre (AU) · ACSC 2020 Health Sector Snapshot
